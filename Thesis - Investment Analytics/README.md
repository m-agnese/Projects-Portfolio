## ðŸ“Š Key Findings & Strategic Impact

The analysis provides actionable strategic insights into how non-financial factors influence capital allocation:

* **Quantifiable Impact of ESG on Capital:** **ESG scores show a significant positive correlation with the *total amount* of VC investment secured.** This confirms that sophisticated investors use ESG factors as a risk/opportunity signal when deploying large sums of capital.
* **Identifying True Drivers over Proxies:** The study found that the **likelihood of securing VC investment** is *not* driven by sustainability scores, but by **stronger economic fundamentals** such as **R&D spending** and **Foreign Direct Investment (FDI)**. This highlights the ability to use complex modeling to identify the *real* leverage points for attracting venture capital.
* **Geographical Strategy:** Found that while high-ESG countries attract more VC-backed firms, the presence of strong **R&D infrastructure** and favorable operational costs plays a critical, non-linear role in determining firm location patterns.
